About Me

Hello there. My name is Mike and I am currently a Research and Teaching assistant at the University of Sheffield where I also work towards a PhD under the supervision of Georg Struth and Harsh Beohar. Before coming to Sheffield I completed a BSc in Computer Science and Mathematics at the University of Manchester, and a MSc in (mathematical) Logic at the Institute for Logic, Language and Computation (Universiteit van Amsterdam).

My current area of research is that of formal language theory where I am particularly interested in regular languages of infinite words and the corresponding automata. I study them using algebra and coalgebra in a mostly categorical setting. Additionally, I explore their connections to logic and how they can be used for the verification and falsification of systems.

When I am not at the office (which happens occasionally), then you will most likely find me hiking the peaks or Sheffield's surroundings, learning languages, spending time with friends or travelling. I shouldn't forget to mention that I like eating (and cooking).
